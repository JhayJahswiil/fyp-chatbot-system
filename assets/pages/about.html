Project Description

The Student Project Supervisor Recommendation System is a cutting-edge solution designed to revolutionize the process of allocating project supervisors to students in educational institutions. This advanced system utilizes state-of-the-art technology and intelligent algorithms to streamline and enhance the supervisor recommendation process.

Objectives

The primary objectives of our project are as follows:

  • 1. Efficiency and Time Savings: The manual allocation of project supervisors can be a time-consuming and cumbersome task. Our system aims to automate and expedite this process, significantly reducing the administrative burden and saving valuable time for both students and staff.
  • 2. Personalized Supervisor Matching: Finding the most suitable project supervisor is vital for the success of student projects. Our system employs sophisticated algorithms that take into account various factors, such as the project topic, supervisor expertise, availability, and past performance. This ensures personalized and optimal matches, enhancing the learning experience for students.
  • 3. Enhanced Collaboration and Support: Effective collaboration between students and supervisors is crucial for project success. Our system facilitates seamless communication and interaction between students and their assigned supervisors, fostering a supportive and productive working relationship.
  • 4. Fairness and Transparency: Our recommendation system ensures fairness and transparency in the allocation process. By leveraging objective criteria and unbiased algorithms, we eliminate biases and favoritism, ensuring equal opportunities for all students to receive appropriate supervision.

Technologies Used

The project harnesses the power of the following technologies:

  • 1. Microsoft Power Virtual Agent (PVA): We have integrated our recommendation system into a user-friendly chat-based interface using Microsoft Power Virtual Agent. This intuitive interface allows students to effortlessly provide their project topics and receive supervisor recommendations.
  • 2. SharePoint: We utilize SharePoint as a centralized database to store crucial information about project supervisors, including their areas of expertise, qualifications, and availability. This data is seamlessly accessed and processed by our system to generate accurate supervisor recommendations.
  • 3. HTML, CSS, and JavaScript: The frontend of our system is built using the trifecta of web technologies: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This ensures a visually appealing and responsive user interface, offering an immersive experience to students interacting with the recommendation system.
  • 4. Sass: We leverage the power of Sass to streamline our CSS workflow and enhance code maintainability. With features like variables, mixins, and nesting, Sass allows us to write clean and modular stylesheets, making it easier to manage the visual aspects of our application.

Limitations

  • 1. Restricted to Computer Science Department: Currently, our system is limited to the Computer Science Department of Ekiti State University. It has been tailored to address the specific needs and requirements of this department. However, there is potential for future expansion to include other departments and disciplines within the university.
  • 2. Dependency on Project Topics: The accuracy and effectiveness of supervisor recommendations heavily rely on the quality and specificity of the project topics provided by students. If students provide vague or incomplete project topics, the system may not be able to generate optimal supervisor matches.
  • 3. Lack of Human Judgment: While our system utilizes intelligent algorithms, it lacks the human judgment and intuition that can be present in manual supervisor allocation. Some factors, such as personal rapport and mentoring style, may not be fully captured by the system.

By harnessing these cutting-edge technologies, our Student Project Supervisor Recommendation System optimizes the allocation process, facilitates personalized matches, and fosters a collaborative environment, ultimately empowering students to excel in their project endeavors.
